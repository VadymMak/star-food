## What are the most important food exhibitions in Europe 2026?

### SIAL Paris — October 19–23, 2026

SIAL Paris is the world's largest food innovation exhibition, held biennially at Paris Le Bourget. The 2026 edition is expected to attract over 7,000 exhibitors from 130+ countries and 300,000+ professional visitors over five days.

**Who exhibits:** Food manufacturers, wholesalers, processors, ingredient suppliers, private label producers. Every major category of food and beverage is represented — from bulk commodity oils to specialty processed foods.

**Who attends:** Retail buyers, food service operators, importers, distributors, and trading companies from across Europe and globally.

**What to look for at SIAL for commodity sourcing:** The Eastern European and Central Asian pavilions are where bulk vegetable oil suppliers, sugar traders, and grain commodity companies concentrate. Ukrainian, Bulgarian, Romanian, and Polish suppliers are typically well represented.

**Practical tips:**

- Register as a trade visitor at least 6 weeks in advance — free for verified professionals
- Request exhibitor lists by product category before arrival
- Schedule meetings with priority suppliers before the event opens — the best suppliers have full calendars by day 2
- Bring your product specifications and volume requirements in writing

## How to prepare for a food exhibition as a buyer?

The difference between a productive trade show and three days of exhausting walking is preparation. Buyers who get the most from food exhibitions follow a clear process before, during, and after the event.

**Before the event:**

1. Download the full exhibitor list (available on event websites 4–8 weeks before opening)
2. Filter by product category — focus only on your sourcing priorities
3. Research your shortlisted exhibitors: website, product range, certifications, countries they export to
4. Send meeting requests to priority suppliers at least 3 weeks before the event — most serious exhibitors schedule back-to-back meetings
5. Prepare a supplier evaluation form: what questions will you ask every supplier to compare them fairly?

**At the event:**

- Spend maximum 20 minutes per initial supplier meeting — enough to assess whether they are worth a follow-up meeting
- Collect product samples with batch documentation, not just marketing brochures
- Ask specifically about: minimum order quantities, lead times, payment terms, certifications available
- Take notes immediately after each meeting — after 15 meetings, details blur together

**After the event:**

- Follow up within 48 hours with your top 3–5 suppliers
- Request samples and full documentation (Certificate of Analysis, certificate of origin, HACCP/ISO certificates)
- Evaluate sample quality before committing to a trial order

## How do exhibitions compare to online sourcing platforms for finding food suppliers?

Both channels have legitimate roles in a professional sourcing strategy. The choice depends on what stage of the supplier relationship you are at.

| Sourcing Channel       | Best for                                                     | Weakness                               |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Food exhibitions       | Initial verification, relationship building, product tasting | Time and travel cost                   |
| Europages, Kompass     | Finding verified EU suppliers, initial contact               | Cannot verify quality without sampling |
| Direct website contact | Following up on known suppliers                              | No comparison mechanism                |
| LinkedIn               | Identifying key contacts, soft relationship building         | Difficult to verify product claims     |
| Trade directories      | Market overview, price benchmarking                          | Often outdated information             |

The most effective sourcing strategy combines all channels: use directories and LinkedIn to identify candidates, contact them online, and verify the best candidates at exhibitions or through facility visits.
