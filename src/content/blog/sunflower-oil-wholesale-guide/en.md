## Types of Sunflower Oil Available for Wholesale

### Refined Sunflower Oil
The most commonly traded type. Refined oil undergoes degumming, neutralization, bleaching, and deodorization. It has a neutral taste, light color, and high smoke point (225°C), making it ideal for cooking, frying, and food manufacturing.

**Key specifications:**
- Acidity: max 0.1%
- Color: light yellow, transparent
- Smoke point: 225°C
- Shelf life: 12-18 months

### Crude (Unrefined) Sunflower Oil
Pressed directly from sunflower seeds without chemical processing. It retains its natural golden color, distinct flavor, and higher vitamin E content. Used mainly in food production as a raw material.

**Key specifications:**
- Acidity: max 2.0%
- Color: dark golden
- Moisture: max 0.15%
- Used for: further refining or specialty products

### High-Oleic Sunflower Oil
A premium variety with oleic acid content above 80%. It offers superior stability at high temperatures, longer shelf life, and a healthier fatty acid profile. Increasingly demanded by HORECA and food manufacturing sectors.

**Key specifications:**
- Oleic acid: 80%+
- Superior frying stability
- Extended shelf life
- Premium pricing (15-25% above standard)

## Quality Grades and Certifications

When purchasing wholesale sunflower oil, look for these certifications:

- **ISO 22000** — Food safety management system
- **HACCP** — Hazard analysis and critical control points
- **Non-GMO** — Genetically modified organism free
- **EU Food Safety Compliance** — Meets European regulations
- **Kosher / Halal** — For specific market requirements

Quality is typically classified by acidity level, peroxide value, color index, and moisture content. Always request a Certificate of Analysis (COA) before placing orders.

## Pricing Factors for Wholesale Sunflower Oil

Several factors affect wholesale sunflower oil prices:

### 1. Raw Material Costs
Sunflower seed prices fluctuate based on harvest yields in Ukraine, Russia, Argentina, and Turkey — the world's largest producers. A poor harvest in any of these countries can significantly impact global prices.

### 2. Refining Costs
Crude oil is cheaper than refined. The refining process adds approximately $50-100 per ton to the final price.

### 3. Packaging
Bulk tanker delivery is the most cost-effective. PET bottles (1L, 3L, 5L) add packaging costs. IBC containers offer a middle ground for medium volumes.

### 4. Delivery Terms (Incoterms)
- **FOB (Free on Board):** Buyer pays shipping from port of origin
- **CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight):** Seller covers shipping to destination port
- **DAP (Delivered at Place):** Seller delivers to buyer's warehouse

### 5. Currency and Market Conditions
Oil is typically priced in USD. Euro/USD exchange rate fluctuations directly affect European buyers.

**Current price range (2026):** $1,050 — $1,250 per metric ton FOB Black Sea ports, depending on type and volume.
