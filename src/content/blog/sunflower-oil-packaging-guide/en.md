## Why Packaging Choice Matters

The right packaging affects your total cost, shelf life, logistics, and target market. Choosing correctly can save thousands per shipment.

## Packaging Options Compared

### PET Bottles (1L, 3L, 5L)
The standard retail format. Clear plastic bottles that consumers recognize on store shelves.

- **Best for:** Retail distribution, small HORECA
- **MOQ:** 1 pallet (~800-1,200 bottles depending on size)
- **Cost impact:** Highest per-liter cost (packaging adds $150-250/ton)
- **Shelf life:** 12-18 months
- **Pros:** Consumer-ready, brand visibility, easy storage
- **Cons:** Higher cost, more storage space per liter

### PET Bottles (10L, 18L)
Larger format for food service and light industrial use.

- **Best for:** Restaurants, cafeterias, small food producers
- **MOQ:** 1 pallet
- **Cost impact:** Medium ($100-150/ton above bulk)
- **Shelf life:** 12-18 months
- **Pros:** Convenient handling, no special equipment needed
- **Cons:** Still more expensive than bulk

### IBC Containers (1,000L)
Intermediate Bulk Containers — reusable plastic containers in a metal cage.

- **Best for:** Medium-volume buyers, food manufacturers
- **MOQ:** 1-20 containers per order
- **Cost impact:** Low-medium ($50-80/ton above bulk)
- **Shelf life:** 12 months
- **Pros:** Reusable, easy to move with forklift, good middle ground
- **Cons:** Requires forklift, return logistics

### Flexitanks (20-24 tons)
Single-use bags fitted inside standard shipping containers. The most popular bulk format.

- **Best for:** Large wholesale, international trade
- **MOQ:** 20-24 tons (one container)
- **Cost impact:** Low ($20-30/ton above FOB base)
- **Shelf life:** 6-9 months (shorter due to single-wall protection)
- **Pros:** Cost-effective, no return logistics, fits standard containers
- **Cons:** Single use, needs pump for unloading, shorter shelf life

### Bulk Tanker (20-40 tons)
Dedicated road tanker trucks for direct delivery.

- **Best for:** Large-volume buyers, refineries, factories
- **MOQ:** 20 tons (full truck)
- **Cost impact:** Lowest (base FOB price)
- **Shelf life:** Immediate use recommended
- **Pros:** Cheapest per liter, direct delivery to storage tank
- **Cons:** Requires storage tanks, pump equipment

## Regulatory Compliance & EU Labeling Requirements

When selecting packaging for sunflower oil and other food products sold within the EU, compliance with food labeling regulations is non-negotiable. All packaged oils must meet **EU Regulation 1169/2011** requirements, which mandate clear labeling of allergens, nutritional information, origin, and best-before dates. Our PET bottle options come pre-compliance with these standards, while private label and bulk options require your own compliance review.

For exports outside the EU, packaging requirements vary significantly. For instance, some Middle Eastern and North African markets require specific language labeling and certifications. Bulgarian origin documentation provides an advantage in these regions due to established trade relationships. When choosing flexitanks or bulk tanker formats for international trade, ensure your logistics partner understands destination-specific requirements — customs delays due to labeling issues can cost weeks and substantial fees.

Additionally, all food-contact plastics must comply with **EU Regulation 10/2011** on plastic materials. This affects PET bottle sourcing; low-quality bottles may not meet migration thresholds for mineral oils and other contaminants. Our suppliers maintain accredited testing, but if you source your own packaging, independent laboratory certification is recommended before scaling up.

## Transportation & Logistics Considerations

Packaging choice directly impacts your supply chain efficiency and costs. PET bottles are ideal for European retail distribution via standard palletized logistics networks — most major distributors accept them without special handling. However, shipping to distant warehouses means higher volumetric weight charges; 1-liter bottles can increase freight costs by 40-60% compared to bulk formats.

IBC containers offer flexibility for mid-sized buyers who lack permanent storage infrastructure. The reusable metal cage protects the plastic bladder during handling, reducing damage risk during multi-modal transport (truck, rail, or container ship). Return logistics typically add 2-3 weeks to your timeline if containers must come back to our Bulgarian facility, though some European buyers arrange local container pools to speed returns.

Flexitanks represent the sweet spot for most wholesale buyers. One 20-foot container holds 20-24 tons of oil and costs roughly the same to ship as a container of lighter goods. The flexitank is fitted inside the container at our facility and pumped out at your warehouse — no special equipment needed beyond a basic pump connection. Transit time from Bulgaria to Western Europe averages 4-6 days by road, with flexitank shelf life of 6-9 months providing adequate buffer for distribution and retail display.

Bulk tanker delivery suits refineries, large food manufacturers, and buyers with dedicated tank storage. These vehicles carry 20-40 tons depending on weight restrictions in your country. Direct tank-to-tank transfer minimizes handling and maximizes product quality retention. Lead times typically require 7-14 days coordination, especially for multi-country shipments, as tanker trucks cannot be used for non-food products without extensive cleaning protocols.

## Shelf Life & Storage Best Practices

Packaging material significantly affects how long sunflower oil maintains quality. Light exposure, temperature fluctuations, and oxygen contact all degrade oil through oxidation. PET bottles offer reasonable UV protection with dark tinting, supporting 12-18 months shelf life in ambient conditions. However, sunflower oil stored in PET bottles should remain below 20°C ideally; warmer warehouse temperatures reduce this to 9-12 months.

Flexitanks and IBC containers provide superior protection because they're opaque and resist temperature swings better in shipping containers. The trade-off is that once opened for pumping, remaining oil must be transferred to sealed storage within hours to prevent oxidation. Many of our buyers establish this workflow with their production teams.

For private label retail products, consider your distribution geography. If selling into Mediterranean or Middle Eastern markets with hot summers, smaller PET bottles (1-3L) are preferable because buyers consume faster and oxidation risk is lower. For Northern European wholesale, 10-18L bottles in temperature-controlled storage can easily reach 18-month shelf life.

## Choosing the Right Format for Your Business Model

Your market segment should guide packaging selection. **Retail supermarket chains** expect PET bottles in Star Food or private label format — typically 1L or 5L sizes. Margins are tighter but volume is consistent. **Food service distributors** and restaurant suppliers often prefer 10-18L bottles because they reduce shelf clutter and replacement frequency; customers accept 2-4 week reorder cycles. **Food manufacturers** using oil as an ingredient typically buy IBC containers or flexitanks to minimize packaging waste and reduce per-unit costs by 30-40%.

If you're scaling a new product line or testing market demand, start with 1-2 pallets of PET bottles to gauge retail response
