## Why Label Design Matters in Food Trading

In the B2B food industry, product labels serve a critical role beyond legal compliance. They communicate professionalism, build brand recognition, and create trust with distributors and retailers. A well-designed label can be the difference between securing a partnership and being passed over.

When we launched the Star Food brand, we knew that investing in professional label design was essential for our European market positioning.

## Working with a Professional Designer

For the Star Food label project, we partnered with [Anastasiia Kolisnyk](https://akillustrator.com), a professional illustrator and designer based in Slovakia. Anastasiia specializes in product packaging, branding, and illustration — making her the perfect fit for our food label needs.

## The Design Process

### Step 1: Brand Brief
We provided Anastasiia with our brand requirements:
- **Brand identity:** Star Food — quality European food products
- **Color palette:** Blue (trust, quality) + Yellow (sunflower, warmth)
- **Target market:** European wholesale buyers and retailers
- **Legal requirements:** EU food labeling regulations, multilingual text
- **Languages on label:** English, Bulgarian, Greek, Polish, Ukrainian

### Step 2: Concept Development
Anastasiia developed several concepts for the label, focusing on:
- The Star Food circular logo as the central element
- A sunflower field imagery to represent the product's natural origin
- Clean, readable typography for nutritional information
- Color-coded sections for easy navigation of label information
- Premium feel that communicates quality to B2B buyers

### Step 3: Refinement and Testing
The initial concepts went through multiple rounds of feedback:
- Adjusted the sunflower imagery for stronger visual impact
- Optimized text sizing for readability at bottle scale
- Ensured all EU-mandated nutritional information was properly positioned
- Verified the design worked across different bottle sizes (1L, 3L, 5L)

### Step 4: Final Production
The final label design includes:
- **Front panel:** Star Food logo, product name, volume, key certifications
- **Back panel:** Complete nutritional information, ingredients, storage conditions
- **Side panels:** Company details, barcode, batch number space
- **Multilingual text:** All required information in 5 languages

## The Result

The Star Food label design has become a key asset in our business development. Distributors consistently comment on the professional appearance of our packaging, and the clear, multilingual labeling makes our products ready for multiple European markets without repackaging.

## What We Learned About Food Label Design

Based on our experience, here are key considerations for anyone developing food product labels for the European market:

1. **Start with regulatory requirements** — EU food labeling regulations are strict. Design around compliance, not the other way around.
2. **Invest in professional design** — B2B buyers judge quality by packaging. A cheap-looking label undermines your product positioning.
3. **Plan for multiple languages** — If you're selling across Europe, design your label to accommodate multilingual text from the start.
4. **Think about shelf presence** — How does your label look next to competitors? Stand out while maintaining professionalism.
5. **Consider all bottle sizes** — The design should scale from 1L PET to 5L without losing impact.

## EU Regulations and Labeling Compliance

When developing labels for food products sold within the European Union, compliance with **EU Regulation 1169/2011** is non-negotiable. This regulation establishes mandatory labeling requirements for all pre-packaged food products, and violations can result in significant fines and product recalls.

Key requirements your label must include:

- **Name of the food product** — Must be clear and accurate (e.g., "Refined Sunflower Oil")
- **List of ingredients** — In descending order by weight, with allergens clearly highlighted
- **Net quantity** — Expressed in liters, kilograms, or grams, depending on the product
- **Date marking** — Either "Best Before" or "Use By" date, depending on shelf life
- **Storage instructions** — Clearly visible storage temperature and conditions
- **Name and address of responsible party** — The manufacturer, importer, or distributor must be identified
- **Country of origin** — For certain products like oils, origin labeling is essential for B2B buyers
- **Nutritional information** — Per 100ml/100g and per serving (if applicable)
- **Allergen declarations** — Any potential allergens must be highlighted

From UB Market's perspective, ensuring full compliance during the design phase prevents costly reprinting and delays in market entry. Our Star Food labels undergo rigorous compliance checks before production to guarantee acceptance across all EU member states.

## Market Impact: Why Professional Labels Drive B2B Sales

The European food wholesale market is highly competitive, with buyers evaluating products based on multiple criteria — price, quality, and presentation. A professionally designed label immediately signals that your company is serious about food safety, quality standards, and market positioning.

When distributors and retailers evaluate new suppliers, the label is often their first impression. In logistics and warehousing environments, clear, professional labeling also reduces operational errors. Retailers appreciate suppliers who understand their markets and provide multilingual packaging that simplifies inventory management across different territories.

For companies like UB Market trading wholesale sunflower oil, sugar, and other food products, label design isn't a cosmetic choice — it's a strategic business decision that affects market penetration, brand credibility, and long-term partnership development.

## Production and Quality Assurance

After the design phase, we partnered with certified label manufacturers to ensure print quality and material durability. Food labels must withstand warehouse conditions, temperature fluctuations, and handling during transport. We use high-grade vinyl and water-resistant printing to guarantee that labels remain legible and professional throughout the product's shelf life in distribution centers across Europe.

Quality control checks are conducted on every production batch to verify:
- Color accuracy and consistency
- Text legibility and correct information placement
- Barcode scanning capability
- Label adhesion to PET bottles
- Compliance with all printed regulatory requirements
